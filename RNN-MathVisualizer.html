Why Tanh? Notice how the "Raw Sum (z)" can grow very large or very negative. If we just passed that number to the next frame, the values would explode to infinity after a few loops. The tanh() function safely "squishes" any incoming number to perfectly fit between -1.0 and 1.0, keeping the network's memory stable over time.

The Time Loop: When you click the Step Time button, you are simulating a video moving to the next frame. The h_t output physically becomes the h_{t-1} input for the next calculation. This is how the AI "remembers" what it saw in the past!
